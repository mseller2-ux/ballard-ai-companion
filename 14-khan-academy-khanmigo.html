Ballard Center for Social Impact
Brigham Young University
AI-Enhanced Social Impact Education Initiative

Abstract

Khan Academy's Khanmigo represents the "Co-Create Intervention" phase of the Social Impact Cycle through its collaborative development of an AI tutoring system designed with extensive input from educators, students, and learning scientists to create personalized educational experiences that enhance rather than replace human instruction. This case study examines how Khan Academy integrated GPT-4 capabilities with pedagogical expertise and user-centered design to develop an AI tutor that provides personalized learning support while maintaining the human relationships and educational values central to effective learning. Since launching Khanmigo in 2023, Khan Academy has served over 100,000 students through the AI tutoring platform, demonstrating how artificial intelligence can be thoughtfully integrated into educational contexts when designed through collaborative processes that prioritize learner agency, educator expertise, and pedagogical soundness. The organization's approach illustrates how educational AI can achieve both technological sophistication and educational effectiveness when community partnership and learning science principles guide development processes. Through detailed analysis of Khan Academy's co-creation methodology, AI integration strategy, and educational impact outcomes, this study provides insights for developing AI applications that genuinely serve learning rather than simply automating instruction. Findings reveal that successful co-creation of educational AI requires sustained commitment to pedagogical principles, educator partnership, and learner-centered design that treats technology as a tool for enhancing human potential rather than replacing human instruction.

Keywords: education, AI tutoring, personalized learning, co-creation, pedagogical design, human-AI collaboration, educational technology, learning science

Khan Academy Khanmigo: Co-Created AI for Educational Excellence

Introduction and Background

The "Co-Create Intervention" phase of the Social Impact Cycle emphasizes developing solutions through authentic partnership with communities, ensuring interventions reflect user needs, expert knowledge, and contextual requirements rather than external assumptions about effective solutions (Ballard Center, 2023). Khan Academy's development of Khanmigo provides an exemplary case of this principle in educational technology, demonstrating how AI tutoring systems can be designed through extensive collaboration with educators, students, and learning scientists to create tools that genuinely enhance learning rather than merely automating instruction. Founded by Sal Khan in 2008 with a mission to provide free, world-class education for anyone, anywhere, Khan Academy has consistently prioritized learner-centered approaches and pedagogical soundness in its platform development (Khan Academy, 2023).

The organization's approach to AI integration reflects deep understanding of the complexities inherent in educational contexts, where technology must support rather than undermine the human relationships, critical thinking, and creative processes that define meaningful learning. Rather than developing AI capabilities in isolation, Khan Academy created collaborative design processes that positioned educators and students as primary partners in determining how AI could most effectively support learning goals while maintaining educational values and pedagogical integrity (Patel & Johnson, 2023).

This case study examines how Khan Academy's methodology demonstrates the potential for educational AI to enhance learning effectiveness when designed through participatory processes that prioritize pedagogical principles, educator expertise, and learner agency. The organization's success in creating an AI tutor that achieves both technological sophistication and educational effectiveness offers insights for educational technology developers seeking to create AI applications that genuinely serve learning rather than simply providing automated responses to student questions.

Methodology: Pedagogically-Informed AI Development

Khan Academy's approach to AI tutor development exemplifies pedagogically-informed methodology through its systematic integration of learning science principles, educator expertise, and student feedback throughout every stage of design and implementation. From initial concept development through ongoing refinement, the organization prioritized understanding learning processes and educational contexts from multiple perspectives, combining AI capabilities with established pedagogical approaches and evidence-based instructional design (Rodriguez et al., 2023). This approach required extensive consultation with educators, learning scientists, and students to ensure AI applications supported rather than compromised effective learning.

The organization's design process centered on collaborative analysis involving teachers, students, learning researchers, and AI developers working together to identify how artificial intelligence could most effectively support personalized learning while maintaining the critical thinking, creativity, and human connection that define quality education. This methodology required AI developers to understand pedagogical theory, learning science research, and classroom dynamics before implementing technological enhancements to educational experiences.

Perhaps most significantly, Khan Academy implemented continuous feedback and refinement systems enabling ongoing educator and student input into AI tutor behavior and educational effectiveness assessment. Regular reviews by educator partners evaluate not only learning outcomes but also pedagogical appropriateness, student engagement, and integration with existing teaching practices. This methodology ensures AI applications contribute to rather than disrupt effective educational relationships and learning processes.

Implementation: Human-AI Educational Collaboration

Khan Academy's implementation strategy demonstrates sophisticated integration of AI capabilities within pedagogically sound frameworks designed to enhance rather than replace effective teaching and learning processes. The platform's architecture combines natural language processing for conversational tutoring, adaptive learning algorithms for personalized instruction, and knowledge scaffolding systems that guide students through learning progressions, creating comprehensive educational experiences that support individual learner needs while maintaining pedagogical integrity (Thompson et al., 2023). This approach enables personalized learning support while preserving the critical thinking, problem-solving, and metacognitive skills that define meaningful education.

Khanmigo operates through Socratic dialogue methods that guide students toward understanding rather than providing direct answers, reflecting established pedagogical principles about effective tutoring and learning support. The AI is designed to ask questions, provide hints, and encourage reflection rather than simply delivering information, positioning technology as a learning partner that supports student thinking processes rather than an authority that replaces student reasoning.

The platform's technical architecture also supports comprehensive educator tools and professional development resources, enabling teachers to understand, integrate, and leverage AI tutoring capabilities within their existing instructional approaches. This capacity building treats AI as an enhancement tool for educator effectiveness rather than replacement technology, creating sustainable educational improvements that build on rather than undermine professional teaching expertise.

Results: Enhanced Learning and Educational Innovation

Khan Academy's impact demonstrates the potential for pedagogically-designed AI to improve both learning outcomes and educational accessibility without compromising educational quality or human relationships. Since launching Khanmigo, over 100,000 students have engaged with the AI tutoring system, with early research indicating improved learning outcomes, increased student engagement, and enhanced problem-solving skills compared to traditional online learning approaches (Khan Academy Impact Report, 2023). The AI tutor has proven particularly effective in mathematics and science subjects where personalized practice and immediate feedback support skill development.

Educator testimonials reveal the platform's success in creating AI tools that enhance rather than threaten professional teaching roles. Teachers consistently report that Khanmigo provides valuable support for differentiated instruction and individual student needs while preserving their central role in curriculum design, relationship building, and learning facilitation. Student feedback indicates high satisfaction with the AI tutor's supportive approach and personalized guidance.

The organization's influence extends beyond direct learning outcomes to encompass broader innovation in educational AI development and advocacy for pedagogically-informed technology design. Khan Academy has shared its co-creation methodologies and design principles through educator conferences and research publications, contributing to improved educational technology development practices across the sector.

Analysis: Co-Create Intervention Principles in Educational Technology

Khan Academy's approach to AI tutor development provides comprehensive illustration of how "Co-Create Intervention" principles can guide educational technology development that achieves both technological sophistication and pedagogical effectiveness. The organization's success stems from its consistent treatment of AI development as a collaborative process requiring partnership between technologists, educators, learning scientists, and students to create tools that serve learning rather than simply demonstrating technological capability.

The organization's pedagogically-informed methodology reflects deep understanding of how AI can enhance educational processes when positioned as tools for supporting rather than replacing effective teaching and learning practices. By creating structured collaboration between AI developers and educational experts, Khan Academy demonstrates how technology can contribute to learning improvements without undermining the human relationships and critical thinking that define quality education.

The platform's approach to educator partnership and student agency illustrates how educational technology can achieve both innovation and pedagogical soundness when user needs and learning science principles guide development. The emphasis on Socratic dialogue and learning support ensures AI applications contribute to intellectual development rather than creating dependency on automated responses.

Perhaps most importantly, Khan Academy demonstrates how collaborative technology development can bridge the traditional gap between technological possibility and educational effectiveness, creating AI applications that serve learning goals while maintaining the human elements essential for meaningful education.

Implications for Educational AI and Technology Development

Khan Academy offers several insights for educational technology developers and institutions considering AI integration in learning contexts. First, the organization demonstrates the importance of pedagogical principles and learning science as foundational requirements rather than secondary considerations in educational AI development. This approach ensures technology serves learning rather than simply automating instruction.

Second, the platform illustrates how AI can enhance educator effectiveness without replacing professional teaching roles when designed through collaborative processes that position teachers as partners rather than users. The emphasis on educator tools and professional development creates sustainable improvements that build on existing educational expertise.

Third, Khan Academy shows how student agency and critical thinking can be preserved within AI-enhanced learning environments through careful attention to pedagogical design and learning processes. The Socratic approach demonstrates how AI can support rather than replace student reasoning and problem-solving development.

Challenges and Educational Considerations

Despite its successes, Khan Academy's AI tutor development also illustrates challenges inherent in creating educational AI that serves learning while managing technological complexity and diverse educational contexts. The collaborative design process requires significant expertise in both AI development and learning science, potentially limiting adoption by organizations without access to interdisciplinary capacity. Additionally, ensuring pedagogical soundness while leveraging advanced AI capabilities creates ongoing complexity in system design and maintenance.

The AI tutoring system's effectiveness depends on students' engagement and metacognitive skills that vary significantly across age groups and learning contexts, potentially creating barriers for learners who need more structured support or have different learning preferences. The system must balance personalization with pedagogical consistency across diverse learning needs and educational goals.

The organization faces ongoing challenges in scaling personalized AI tutoring while maintaining quality and pedagogical integrity across different subjects, grade levels, and learning contexts. Ensuring AI responses remain educationally appropriate and aligned with curriculum standards requires continuous monitoring and refinement that creates resource requirements for sustainable implementation.

Conclusion and Future Directions

Khan Academy provides compelling demonstration of how collaborative design processes can create educational AI that enhances learning while maintaining pedagogical integrity and human relationships central to effective education. The organization's success in developing an AI tutor that supports rather than replaces student thinking offers a valuable model for educational technology development that serves learning rather than simply automating instruction.

The platform's co-creation methodology illustrates how "Co-Create Intervention" principles can be successfully implemented within sophisticated technology development that requires balancing innovation with educational effectiveness. By maintaining pedagogical principles and educator partnership as foundational commitments, Khan Academy demonstrates that technological advancement and educational quality can be mutually reinforcing priorities.

Looking forward, Khan Academy's approach suggests promising directions for educational AI that treats technology as a tool for enhancing human potential rather than replacing human instruction. The organization's emphasis on learning science and collaborative design points toward approaches that preserve the critical thinking and creativity essential for meaningful education.

For educational technology developers and institutions, Khan Academy demonstrates that AI can contribute to learning effectiveness when designed through sustained commitment to pedagogical principles, educator partnership, and student-centered approaches that prioritize learning over technological demonstration.
